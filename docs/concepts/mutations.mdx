title: "Mutations"

A **Mutation** is the atomic unit of graph change in SiafuDB. Every write produces one or more mutations. Mutations are the currency of the Graph Sync Protocol — they are what gets logged, shipped, transformed, and applied on the receiving end.

## Shape

A mutation carries:

- A **UUID** — unique identifier.
- A **source identity** — who authored this change.
- An **operation type** — `vertex-create`, `vertex-update`, `vertex-delete`, `edge-create`, `edge-update`, `edge-delete`.
- A **target** — the vertex or edge being changed.
- A **payload** — the new state (or the diff).
- A **vector clock** — for causal ordering across fragments.
- An optional **cryptographic signature** — for provenance.

## Batches

Mutations group into atomic `MutationBatch` structures. A batch applies all-or-nothing. Within a batch, causal ordering is preserved. Across batches, vector clocks determine ordering.

## Change Data Capture

The engine emits mutations through Change Data Capture (CDC) — this is what GrafeoDB gives SiafuDB for free. CDC is the hook the sync engine listens on. Every committed mutation becomes a candidate for propagation.
