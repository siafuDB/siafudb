Every major platform — Facebook, Google, Amazon, Netflix — started with relational databases and spent billions building graph layers on top. The relational model was designed in 1970 for accounting ledgers. The human brain is a graph. AI systems reason over graphs. The world is connected through relationships, not rows.

Server-side graph databases (Neo4j, JanusGraph, TigerGraph) solved this for the cloud. But the **device**, the **edge**, and the **decentralised web** have been left behind — still running SQLite, still thinking in tables.

SiafuDB brings graph-native intelligence to every environment where data actually lives:

- **On your phone.** Your AI assistant reasons over your personal knowledge graph locally, even offline.
- **At the edge.** Cloudflare Workers and Durable Objects hold cached regional subgraphs via WASM.
- **In your pod.** Sovereign personal data stored as a graph, cryptographically bound to your identity.
- **In the browser.** WASM-compiled graph engine running client-side with zero server dependency.

## The Four Promises

**Graph-native.** Relationships are first-class. No JOIN tables. Traversal in microseconds through index-free adjacency.

**SQLite-simple.** One file. Zero config. Embed in any app, in any language binding.

**Sync-ready.** Graph Sync Protocol with privacy transformations — PII stripping, anonymisation — applied per destination, not per app.

**Sovereign.** Cryptographic identity. Your data, your graph, your device. Personal and network fragments physically separate on disk.

## What SiafuDB is not

SiafuDB is not ArangoDB-in-Rust. It is not a multi-model database in the old-school sense where four engines sit behind one API. The graph is the storage; KV, document, and time-series are convenience access patterns on top of graph primitives.

The graph is the upgrade path, not the entry requirement.
